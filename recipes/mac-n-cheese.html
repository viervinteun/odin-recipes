Stove Top Macaroni and Cheese

A lot of mac and cheese recipes out there just makes them extra fancy with a lot of ingredients. This is the base of the base, you can expand it as you like or keep it as is!

Description

Quick, easy, and tasty macaroni and cheese dish. All in a single pot.

Ingredients

  • Elbow macaroni, but any other bite-sized pasta will do
  • Butter or margarine
  • Flour
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Milk
  • Cheddar cheese, not pre-shredded

    • Optional Ingredients:
  • Dried or fresh minced parsley
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Shred the cheese. Do this first and foremost! Pre-shredded cheese doesn't taste as good as freshly shredded, but they'll do the job in a pinch.
  2. Boil the macaroni. Bring a pot to a boil, add a pinch of salt to water, and cook pasta for about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Strain your pasta. Make sure to leave a little pasta water in the pot. We need that starch!
  4. Add butter, then milk, cheese and flour. Turn the head to medium-low. Butter first, about a tablespoon of it. Stir until melted. Add a cup of milk, cold, and then the cheese. Quickly add a tablespoon of flour after and constantly stir.
  5. Final touches, salt and black pepper. Give your dish a bit of a taste. Adjust the flavor to your liking with black pepper and salt (if needed). This is when you should add in the mozzarella cheese and minced parsley. Mix everything until nicely incorporated together.
  6. Plate and serve. If you choose to top with parmesan, dust it here. You can use a bowl or a plate, the only difference is that putting it in a plate will make it cool faster, which might be important to some people and vice versa.

    7. Look doesn't really matter, the taste does!

Note:
If you are vegan, use margarin and cold oatmilk (or other substitute milk, this one works best) and add double or more the flour to the mixture. Use no cheese. Give it a bit more salt. It should work!
