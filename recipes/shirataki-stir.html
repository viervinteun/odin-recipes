Shirataki Noodle Stir Fry

One of those rare dishes that takes more energy to digest than what it gives. Perfect for when you want to be a super greedy eater but already ate way too much for the day. Please never eat this on an empty stomach, that's not healthy.

Description

Quick, delicious, guilt-free meal. Easy cooking after preparations, everything into a single frying pan or wok!

Ingredients

    Bare bones:
  • Shirataki noodles
  • Garlic
  • Cornstarch
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Half tablespoon of sesame oil (can be substituted with other oils)
  • Sugar or mirin
  • Salt and black/white pepper

    • Add or Remove as you'd like:
    (Cut into halves, thin strips, dice or smalls)
  • Baby corns
  • Bean sprouts
  • Broccolis
  • Cabbage
  • Carrots
  • Canned or fresh button mushrooms
  • Green onion
  • Green beans
  • Onion
  • Red onion
  • Sugar snap peas

Steps

  1. Heat the wok or frying pan, put oil in, wait until oil swishes like water.
  2. Add garlic in first, stir for a moment or two.
  3. Toss the noodles, a teaspoon of salt and pepper.
  4. Dump everything else into the pan or wok. Use spatula to toss it around and mix evenly.
  5. mix a spoon of cornstarch with cold water until it becomes liquid, mix with the stir fry.
  6. Add soy and oyster sauce to taste. Start with half a tablespoon of each and keep tasting and adding little by little until it taste just right. This may include salt and pepper.
  7. Make sure everything is nicely cooked before plating.

Simple cooking steps isn't it? Nothing like a cooking that's mostly just throwing everything into the pan after cutting them.
